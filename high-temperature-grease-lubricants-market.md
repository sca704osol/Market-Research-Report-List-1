<p><p>The high temperature grease and lubricants market is categorized into soap thickener and non-soap thickener types. Soap thickeners, commonly made from lithium, calcium, or sodium, provide excellent stability and water resistance, making them ideal for high-temperature applications. Non-soap thickeners, such as polyurea or clay, offer alternative properties like better shear stability and resistance to oxidation. Both types cater to industries requiring lubricants that can withstand extreme temperatures while maintaining performance, ensuring machinery longevity and efficiency.</p></p>
