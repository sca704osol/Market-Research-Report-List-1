<p><p>Adipose tissue-derived stem cell therapy utilizes stem cells harvested from adipose (fat) tissue for regenerative medicine. The market is primarily divided into two types: autologous stem cells and allogeneic stem cells. Autologous stem cells are derived from the patient's own body, minimizing rejection risks and enhancing compatibility. In contrast, allogeneic stem cells are sourced from donors, allowing for broader application but potentially increasing immunological challenges. Both types are employed in treating various conditions, including orthopedic, cardiovascular, and cosmetic issues.</p></p>
