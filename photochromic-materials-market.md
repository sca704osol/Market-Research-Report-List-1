<p><ul><li>T-type</li><li>P-type</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Photochromic materials are classified primarily into three types: T-type, P-type, and others. T-type materials change color in response to UV light and typically revert to their original state when the light source is removed, making them useful in eyewear and sunglasses. P-type materials, in contrast, respond to visible light, offering applications in smart windows and variable tint displays. Other materials include those tailored for specific applications like sensors or coatings, expanding the versatility of photochromic technologies in various industries.</p></p>
