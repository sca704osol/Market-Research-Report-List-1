Right-handed quartz oscillators are the most commonly used, characterized by a positive optical rotation and good stability, making them ideal for various electronic applications. In contrast, left-handed quartz oscillators possess a negative optical rotation, offering unique properties that can enhance performance in specific scenarios. Both types cater to different requirements in frequency stability, accuracy, and energy efficiency, serving diverse sectors such as telecommunications and consumer electronics.</p></p>
