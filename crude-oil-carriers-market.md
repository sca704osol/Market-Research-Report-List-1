<p><strong>The Crude Oil Carriers Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>VLCC/ULCC</li><li>Suezmax</li><li>Aframax</li><li>Panamax</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Crude oil carriers are categorized based on size and cargo capacity. VLCC (Very Large Crude Carrier) and ULCC (Ultra Large Crude Carrier) can transport large volumes, typically over 200,000 deadweight tons, making them ideal for long-haul routes. Suezmax ships, able to transit the Suez Canal, usually range from 120,000 to 200,000 deadweight tons. Aframax carriers, smaller than Suezmax, typically transport 80,000 to 120,000 tons, while Panamax vessels, designed for the original Panama Canal size, carry around 50,000 to 80,000 tons. Each type serves distinct trade routes and operational efficiencies.</p></p>
