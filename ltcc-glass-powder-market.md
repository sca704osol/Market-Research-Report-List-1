<p><p>The LTCC (Low-Temperature Co-fired Ceramic) glass powder market is divided into two main types: Low-K and High-K. Low-K materials have a low dielectric constant, making them suitable for applications requiring minimal signal loss and reduced crosstalk, often used in RF and microwave devices. In contrast, High-K materials possess a higher dielectric constant, allowing for greater capacitance and integration in miniaturized electronic components, enhancing energy storage capabilities. Each type fulfills specific needs in advanced electronics and telecommunications.</p></p>
