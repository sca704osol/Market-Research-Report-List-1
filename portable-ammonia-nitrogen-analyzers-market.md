<p><p>The portable ammonia nitrogen analyzers market features two primary types: colorimetric methods and electrode methods. Colorimetric methods rely on chemical reactions that produce a colored solution, allowing for quantification of ammonia levels through light absorption. In contrast, electrode methods use ion-selective electrodes that measure changes in electrical potential to determine ammonia concentration. Both methods offer portability and ease of use, catering to various applications such as environmental monitoring and wastewater analysis, ensuring efficient and accurate ammonia nitrogen detection in the field.</p></p>
