<p><p>Bruton Tyrosine Kinase (BTK) inhibitors are categorized into two main types: first-generation and second-generation. First-generation BTK inhibitors, such as ibrutinib, primarily target BTK but may have off-target effects, leading to varied side effects and potential resistance. Second-generation inhibitors, like acalbrutinib and zanubrutinib, have been designed to offer improved selectivity for BTK, reducing off-target interactions and adverse effects. This differentiation enhances their efficacy and tolerability, catering to the evolving treatment landscape for hematological malignancies.</p></p>
