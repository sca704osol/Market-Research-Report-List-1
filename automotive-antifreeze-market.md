<p><strong>The Automotive Antifreeze Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Ethylene Glycol</li><li>Propylene Glycol</li><li>Glycerin</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive antifreeze market comprises three main types: ethylene glycol, propylene glycol, and glycerin. Ethylene glycol is the most common antifreeze, known for its effective heat transfer properties and lower cost; however, it is toxic. Propylene glycol serves as a safer alternative, suitable for food-grade applications, though it is pricier. Glycerin, derived from plant sources, is eco-friendly and non-toxic but less effective in extreme temperatures. Each type serves specific needs based on cost, toxicity, and environmental impact.</p></p>
