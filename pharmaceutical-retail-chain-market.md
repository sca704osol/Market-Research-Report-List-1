<p><strong>The Pharmaceutical Retail Chain Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>RC(Regular Chain)</li><li>FC(Franchise Chain)</li><li>VC(Voluntary Chain)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Pharmaceutical retail chains can be categorized into three main types: </p><p>1. **Regular Chain (RC)**: These are owned and operated by a single corporation, ensuring consistent branding and pricing across locations. </p><p>2. **Franchise Chain (FC)**: In this model, individual franchises are owned by different operators but follow the brand and operational guidelines of the parent company, allowing for localized management while benefiting from brand recognition.</p><p>3. **Voluntary Chain (VC)**: This is a cooperative arrangement where independent pharmacies collaborate for purchasing and marketing benefits, sharing resources while maintaining individual ownership and identity.</p></p>
