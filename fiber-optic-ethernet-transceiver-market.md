<p><p>The fiber optic Ethernet transceiver market features various types catering to diverse networking needs. SFP (Small Form-factor Pluggable) supports data rates up to 1 Gbps, while SFP+ enhances performance to 10 Gbps. QSFP (Quad Small Form-factor Pluggable) and its enhanced version, QSFP+, can handle 40 Gbps and 100 Gbps, respectively, ideal for high-density applications. XFP (10 Gbps) serves longer distances, and CXP (12x10 Gbps) is designed for high-bandwidth applications, accommodating emerging demands in data centers and telecommunications.</p></p>
