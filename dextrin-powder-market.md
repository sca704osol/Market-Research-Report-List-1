<p><p>The dextrin powder market comprises three primary types: yellow dextrin, white dextrin, and brown dextrin. Yellow dextrin is characterized by its moderate viscosity and is commonly used in food processing and adhesives. White dextrin, known for its high solubility and less color, finds applications in pharmaceuticals and food industries as a thickening agent. Brown dextrin, derived from the partial hydrolysis of starch, is used primarily in the production of snacks and as a dietary fiber supplement. Each type has distinct applications based on its properties.</p></p>
